# LETRANBOT-FE TOP VIEW
The top view maps out the symmetrical layout and distribution of the robotic components. From this overhead perspective, the rectangular geometry of the frame is fully exposed, showcasing the separation between the rear control deck and the front steering and sensing modules. This angle provides the clearest of the cable routing as the flat wires fan out from the hub's connection ports to interface with the hardware elements below.

# LETRANBOT-FE FRONT VIEW
The front view, highlights the primary sensor array of the robotic smart car, featuring an illuminated light and color sensor positioned at the leading edge of the chassis to detect paths, lines, or obstacles. This perspective offers a clear look at the front axle geometry, the wide-set light-blue wheels, and the low-profile Technic structural frames. White ribbon cables neatly route backward from the front-facing sensor and the central steering mechanism toward the main processing hub positioned in the background.

# LETRANBOT-FE REAR VIEW
The rear view, centers directly on the main programmable hub which serves as the central brain of the vehicle. This perspective highlights the data ports on the face of the yellow-and-white hub casing, an active orange LED matrix display on top, and the underlying motor assembly responsible for driving the rear wheels. The stance of the light-blue rear wheels is prominently featured, illustrating how the heavier electronic components are balanced securely over the drive axle.

# LETRANBOT-FE SIDE VIEW
The side profile view in provides a comprehensive look at the smart car’s elongated wheelbase and modular structural layout. It shows how the main processing hub is elevated on a magenta framework at the rear, connecting via a black-and-blue skeletal frame to the mid-mounted actuator and the front-mounted sensor apparatus. The loose loops of the white ribbon cables display the point-to-point wiring system that manages the data and power distribution across the entire length of the chassis.
